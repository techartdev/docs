title: "Communities & Visibility"
description: "How SCP2P bounds discovery and creates trusted local networks"
---

A core design philosophy of SCP2P is the intentional absence of a "global search" or global autodiscovery model. Global models inevitably attract spam, SEO manipulation, and mass surveillance.

Instead, SCP2P bounds wide-area discovery using **Communities** and explicit **Share Visibility**.

## The Community Model

A Community in SCP2P is essentially a specialized Share that acts as a decentralized directory. It provides a boundary for discovery.

### Joining a Community
- **Explicit Join:** Communities are not auto-discovered. To join a community, a user must obtain an Invite Link or manually input a valid `ShareId` and `share_pubkey` for that community.
- **Cryptographic Binding:** The `share_pubkey` ensures that the community identity is cryptographically verified, preventing impersonation.
- **Participant Probing:** Once a node joins a community, it can probe its reachable peers to discover other active participants who are also members of that specific community.

### What Communities Provide
When you are a member of a community, you gain two primary capabilities:
1. **Participant Discovery:** You learn the `PeerAddr` (IP/Port/Relay) of other members, enriching your local PEX and routing tables.
2. **Public Share Discovery:** You can browse Shares that other members have explicitly published *to that community*.

## Share Visibility

When a publisher creates a Share (a catalog of files), they must define its visibility in the Manifest. 

There are two visibility modes:

### 1. `private`
- **Requires Exact ID:** A private Share cannot be discovered by browsing. Another user can only subscribe to it if they are given the exact `ShareId`.
- **Use Case:** Personal backups, direct sharing with specific friends or collaborators, or highly sensitive internal distributions.

### 2. `public`
- **Community Browsable:** A public Share is announced to the communities the publisher is a part of.
- **Direct Subscription:** Other members browsing the community can see the Share's metadata (Title, Description) and choose to subscribe to it directly with a single click.
- **Use Case:** Open-source software distribution, public neighborhood bulletin boards, or general community file sharing.

## Search is Always Local

Regardless of whether a Share is `public` or `private`, **search remains strictly local**.

When a user performs a search query (e.g., "vacation photos"), the node *only* queries the local SQLite database. The local database only indexes the Manifests of Shares the user has explicitly chosen to subscribe to.

1. **You join a Community.** (You discover peers).
2. **You browse the Community's public Shares.** (You see what is available).
3. **You subscribe to Shares you care about.** (Their Manifests sync to your machine).
4. **You search.** (Results only come from your curated subscriptions).

This architecture ensures that discovery is bounded, search is instant and spam-free, and metadata never leaks to an untrusted central server.
